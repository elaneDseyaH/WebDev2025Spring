What Are Cookies?

In web development, a cookie is a small text file stored in a user's browser by a website. It allows websites to remember information about a user's visit, such as preferences, login status, or items in a shopping cart.

Why Cookies Exist

HTTP—the protocol websites use—is stateless. Cookies solve this by storing data locally and allowing continuity between sessions.

Key Properties

  • Name & Value: Stored as key-value pairs.
  • Expiration: Time-based control of lifespan.
  • Domain & Path: Defines where cookies apply.
  • Secure & HttpOnly: Controls access and security.

Types of Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary, deleted after session.
  • Persistent Cookies: Long-lived, for preferences.
  • First-party Cookies: From the current site.
  • Third-party Cookies: From outside domains (e.g., ads).

Source: Devopedia

Example

When you log into a website and remain logged in the next day, that’s often due to a persistent cookie storing your session token.

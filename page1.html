What Are Cookies?

In web development, a cookie is a small text file stored in a user's browser by a website. It allows websites to remember information about a user's visit, such as preferences, login status, or items in a shopping cart.

Why Cookies Exist

HTTP—the protocol websites use—is stateless. This means the server doesn’t "remember" anything about a user's previous visits. Cookies solve this by storing data locally and allowing continuity between sessions.

Key Properties of Cookies

  • Name & Value: Every cookie stores data as a key-value pair.
  • Expiration: Cookies can be set to expire after a time period.
  • Domain & Path: These control where cookies are valid.
  • Secure & HttpOnly: Flags that control access and security.

Types of Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary, deleted when the browser is closed.
  • Persistent Cookies: Stay stored even after the browser is closed.
  • First-party Cookies: Set by the site you’re visiting.
  • Third-party Cookies: Set by domains other than the one you’re visiting (typically advertisers).

Real-World Example

When you log into Gmail and stay logged in even after closing your browser, a persistent cookie remembers your session. If you use a shopping site like Amazon, your cart remains saved due to cookies.

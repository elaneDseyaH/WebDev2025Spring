How Cookies Are Used

1. Session Management

Cookies store identifiers to help websites remember users between pages. This is essential for things like staying logged in or keeping a shopping cart active.

2. Personalization

Sites use cookies to save settings like dark mode, preferred language, or region-specific content. This makes the user experience smoother and more personal.

3. Analytics and Tracking

Cookies track how users navigate a website. Tools like Google Analytics use them to measure engagement and optimize layout, content, or performance.

4. Advertising

Third-party cookies help advertisers track browsing history across multiple websites to serve personalized ads. This is where cookies intersect with privacy concerns.

Source: GeeksforGeeks

Privacy & Compliance

  • GDPR (EU): Requires opt-in consent before setting cookies.
  • CCPA (California): Gives users the right to know and opt out of data collection.
  • ePrivacy Directive: Sites must explain cookie usage up front.
